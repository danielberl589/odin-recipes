Couscouma Chapati recipe

Marduf, image from comoresinfo.com

Description

Marduf, or Couscouma as it is called in Comoros Island, is a delicious and soft dish originated from southern asia, then spread to East Africa. Usually served with beef or chicken sauce, or served alone for brekfast as preference varies.

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Ghee
  • Salt
  • Flour

Steps

  1. Poor the Floor in a container
  2. Add salt
  3. Melt the ghee
  4. Add the melted ghee in the container
  5. Add water
  6. Mix all the ingredients to obtain an homogeneous mixture
  7. Leave it to rest for 15 mins
  8. Use a rolling pin to shape Marduf in round
  9. Once you obtain a round shape, put it in a frying pan
  10. Add a some oil and keep turning your marduf until you obtain a golden like color on both side. Use mild fire

    11. Enjoy!
